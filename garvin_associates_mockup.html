About David

Depth. Perspective.
A Grounded Presence.

Many people come to therapy when something in their life no longer feels sustainable — patterns repeating in relationships, stress that never turns off, or a quiet sense of disconnection from the person they want to be.

I bring more than 40 years of experience in behavioral health and social work, including nationally recognized work in domestic violence intervention and relationship dynamics. My approach is thoughtful, collaborative, and grounded. Clients often describe me as calm, direct, and able to hold complex conversations without judgment.

I work with adults navigating anxiety, depression, anger, relationship conflict, trauma, and major life transitions — and I bring that same depth of experience directly into every session.

Areas of Practice

How I Can Help

I work with men, women, couples, and families who want to better understand themselves, strengthen their relationships, and move forward with greater clarity and integrity.

For Individuals — Men

A direct, respectful space to examine the patterns and pressures shaping your life — and develop the emotional clarity to move forward with confidence and integrity.

For Individuals — Women

Grounded in deep respect for resilience and survivorship, this work helps women reconnect with clarity, confidence, and their own sense of agency and voice.

For Couples

A structured, supportive space where both partners can step back, understand what is happening beneath the conflict, and build healthier ways of communicating and relating.

For Families

Family therapy looks at the larger patterns within the family system, helping each member feel heard while creating new ways of communicating and resolving conflict.

Executive Therapy

A confidential space for professionals and leaders to think clearly about their lives, relationships, and direction — without quick fixes or surface-level advice.
